# Gap Analysis: OfficialWho vs. Wikipedia

**Date**: 2026-07-06
**Companion docs**: `PLATFORM_VISION.md` (positioning), `GAP_ANALYSIS_MARQUIS.md` (the other flank)
**Frame**: Marquis is the competitor for the *wallet*. Wikipedia is the competitor for the
*idea of a biography page*. Different fight, different weapons.

---

## Who They Are

The world's reference of record. ~7M English articles, ~1.5M+ of them biographies.
Operated by the nonprofit Wikimedia Foundation (~$180M/yr in donations). The de facto
answer to "who is this person?" for search engines, AI models, and humans.

**Their assets**
- **Total SEO dominance**: a Wikipedia page ranks #1–3 for virtually any name it covers;
  Google Knowledge Panels are built from Wikipedia/Wikidata
- Institutional trust built over 25 years; citation-based verifiability
- A volunteer editor army (declining, but still ~40K active English editors)
- Free forever; no ads; nonprofit halo
- The default source for every LLM and voice assistant

**Their structural constraints (our entire opportunity)**
- **Notability policy**: subjects need significant coverage in independent published
  sources. This *excludes ~99.99% of humanity by design* — the retired lieutenant, the
  choir director, the union electrician can never have a page
- **Neutral point of view**: pages must be dispassionate. No honor, no pride, no voice
  of the family — by policy
- **No subject control**: the person (or family) has no authorship; editing your own
  page is discouraged; errors persist; deletion debates are public and brutal
- **Text-era product**: one thumbnail, no video, no timeline UI, no design — a 2004
  reading experience by intent
- **Editor demographics**: ~85% male, skewing Western/technical → systemic coverage
  gaps for women, minorities, community figures, non-Western lives (well-documented)
- **Deletionism**: thousands of biographies erased per year as "non-notable" —
  every deletion is a grieving family we can serve

## What Wikipedia Proves (learn from them)

1. **People need a canonical page per human.** Wikipedia is the proof that "the page
   about a person" is one of the most valuable objects on the internet.
2. **Community proof-of-work scales.** Volunteer citation-checking built the most
   trusted site on earth. Our co-sign + objection model is the honor-culture analog.
3. **Structured data wins distribution.** Wikidata feeds Google/Alexa/LLMs. We should
   emit schema.org `Person` markup from day one so honoree pages enter Knowledge
   Graph territory for names Wikipedia will never cover.
4. **Neutrality is their moat and their ceiling.** We must never fight them on
   neutral reference — we win on everything neutrality forbids: love, pride, honor.

## The Attack Vectors (where we win)

1. **The uncovered 99.99%** — ~60M US honor-events/year vs. ~10K new Wikipedia bios/month.
   Zero overlap in practice: if Wikipedia would take the page, the family doesn't need us;
   if we'd take it, Wikipedia won't. It's a complement shaped like a competitor.
2. **The SEO land grab** — "James Walker Marshall High School Chicago veteran" has NO
   existing result. Millions of name+context queries where an OfficialWho page would be
   the only structured, photo-rich answer. Every published honoree = a new URL Wikipedia
   structurally cannot contest. (Requires: public listed pages indexed, schema.org
   Person, per-honoree OG cards — all in the sprint plan.)
3. **Deletion refugees** — families whose Wikipedia page was deleted as non-notable are
   pre-qualified, pre-motivated submitters. Tasteful content play: "Deleted from
   Wikipedia? Your community disagrees."
4. **The bias gap** — Wikipedia's documented under-coverage of women, Black communities,
   and non-Western lives is our charter cohort's home turf (gospel, HBCU, steppers,
   Divine Nine — already in the category system).
5. **Living voice** — video, audio ("hear their voice"), family storytelling: things a
   neutral encyclopedia can never host.

## Positioning (vs. both flanks)

> Wikipedia decides who's notable. Marquis decides who can pay.
> **OfficialWho lets communities decide who mattered — and proves it.**

## Risks

- **Don't cosplay as an encyclopedia.** The moment we claim neutrality we inherit
  Wikipedia's obligations without their editors. We are a hall of honor — say so.
- **Notability-free means fraud-burden is ours.** Their notability rule is also a spam
  filter; our equivalents (attestation, 10 witnesses, objections, audit logs) must hold.
- **Permanence skepticism**: "startups die, Wikipedia doesn't." The Legacy Promise
  (export + print + continuity plan) is the answer; make it a public page.
- **Don't disparage Wikipedia** in marketing — it's beloved. "They have rules; we have
  witnesses" is admiration-adjacent, not attack.
