# Platform Vision — The Graphical Successor to the Biography Page

**Date**: 2026-07-06
**Frame**: "TRS-80 vs PS5" — Wikipedia is the text era of biography. OfficialWho is the cinematic era.

---

## The Wikipedia Comparison

| | Wikipedia | OfficialWho |
|---|---|---|
| **Medium** | Text + one thumbnail | Cinematic hero imagery, visual timelines, tribute walls, world map |
| **Who qualifies** | "Notability" — press coverage required; excludes 99.99% of humanity | Anyone with a verified story and people who'll co-sign it |
| **Who writes it** | Anonymous editors; subject has no say | Family, colleagues, community — the people who were there |
| **Emotional register** | Neutral point of view (by policy) | Honor, pride, legacy (by design) |
| **Proof model** | Citations to published sources | Verification ladder (truth) + co-sign ladder (community weight) |
| **Business model** | Donations | Honor products (submission, hero image, prints) |
| **The page itself** | Looks the same for Einstein and a local mayor | Looks like a movie poster for a retired firefighter |

**The wedge**: Wikipedia structurally *cannot* serve the retired police lieutenant, the
church choir director, the first-gen graduate — notability rules forbid it. That's not
their failure; it's their scope. Our scope is everyone whose community says they matter.
There are ~10,000 new Wikipedia biographies a month; there are ~60M US adults whose
retirement, service milestone, or graduation happens *every year*.

**The bar**: every profile must look like it was art-directed. If a profile page could be
mistaken for a Wikipedia stub, we failed. PS5 means: hero portrait (paid product), color
pulled from the honoree's institution (existing dynamic-branding engine), serif display
type, animated timeline, tribute wall, map pin, co-sign counter ticking up.

## Visual-First Product Pillars

1. **Hero image is the page** — the $29 cinematic portrait isn't an add-on; it's the
   difference between a database row and a legacy. Free pages get a dignified branded
   treatment (institution colors + typography), never a gray placeholder.
2. **Timeline as film strip** — milestones rendered as scenes with era-appropriate
   treatment, not a bulleted list.
3. **Tribute wall as guestbook** — co-signs and tributes visible, counted, celebrated.
4. **Every page is a shareable artifact** — per-honoree OG cards generated from the
   hero image (next/og), so profiles travel as posters, not links.

## Co-Sign Ladder — Community Weight (Chip's mechanic)

Wikipedia has citations. We have **co-signers**: real people who put their name on
someone's legacy. One click ("I witness this legacy") + a relationship tag
(family / served with / classmate / taught by / community).

| Co-signs | Badge | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| 10+ | **CO-SIGNED** | Blue seal — the entry bar; profile leaves "pending" feel |
| 50+ | **COMMUNITY HONORED** | Silver seal + featured eligibility boost |
| 100+ | **CENTURY HONOREE** | Gold seal + auto-nominated for weekly Featured |
| 1,000+ | **LEGEND** | Platinum/red seal + permanent Hall of Legends placement |

**Why it converts friends & family** (the real engine):
- Co-signing requires a free account + verified email → every co-sign is an acquisition
- Co-signers instantly earn **Honor Number 1°** → immediate bragging rights + share moment
- Rally dynamics: "Dad's at 87 co-signs — 13 more to Century" is a family group-chat
  campaign that we don't have to run
- Every co-signer is a warm lead for their *own* honoree submission ($19) — the person
  most moved by honoring is the next submitter

**Integrity (badges of scale ≠ badges of truth)**:
- Co-sign count NEVER substitutes for identity verification — two separate badge families,
  visually distinct (truth = check, scale = seal)
- Verified accounts' co-signs count fully; unverified count 0 until email-verified
- Velocity caps + graph heuristics (device/IP clustering, reciprocal-ring detection)
- Relationship tags feed the Honor Number graph — fraud pollutes both, so both share
  one anti-abuse pipeline
- Co-signs are free, forever. Charging for them would be Marquis with extra steps.
